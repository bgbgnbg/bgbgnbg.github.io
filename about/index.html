About Me

Short Bio

I am an incoming Research Scientist at the National Center of Technology Innovation for Pigs. I received my Ph.D. degree in Agricultural Electrification and Automation from Northwest A&F University in 2024.

My research focuses on developing advanced computer vision algorithms and robotic perception systems for smart pig farming automation. Specifically, I specialize in:

  • Computer Vision and Deep Learning for Livestock Monitoring
  • Robotic Perception Systems for Agricultural Automation
  • Intelligent Visual Sensing in Confined Animal Environments
  • Multi-modal Data Fusion for Precision Livestock Farming

Research Interests

My research lies at the intersection of computer vision, robotics, and precision livestock farming. I develop innovative visual perception algorithms and robotic systems that enable:

  • Real-time monitoring and analysis of pig behavior and health
  • Autonomous navigation and environmental perception for agricultural robots
  • Deep learning-based solutions for livestock phenotyping
  • Vision-guided robotic systems for automated farm management

Current Focus:

  • Developing robust computer vision algorithms for challenging indoor farming environments
  • Designing novel deep learning architectures for real-time animal monitoring
  • Integrating multi-modal sensing for enhanced robotic perception in agricultural settings
